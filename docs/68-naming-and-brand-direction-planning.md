# Naming and Brand Direction Planning

## Planning Status

This is a planning document only.

No name is finalised by this task. No domain is purchased. No logo or branding is created. The goal is to define naming criteria and brand direction before availability checks.

## Why Naming Matters Now

The platform is moving from an internal Clean Eats build toward a possible commercial SaaS product.

A good name needs to work for:

- public website
- tenant subdomains
- platform admin/control centre
- future sales conversations
- future client onboarding
- professional SaaS positioning

## Current Working Names

Current working names:

- Food Prod Hub
- Clean Eats Hub

Food Prod Hub is functional and descriptive, but may feel internal/dev-like.

Clean Eats Hub is tenant-specific and should not become the global platform name.

## Brand Positioning

The product is:

A modular operations platform for food manufacturers and production businesses.

Core themes:

- production control
- food operations
- inventory and traceability
- costings
- QA
- workflow visibility
- manufacturing clarity
- tenant-specific HUBs
- practical operational software, not generic bloated ERP

Positioning statement draft:

"An industry-focused operations hub for food production businesses, bringing products, costings, production, inventory, QA and reporting into one connected system."

## Naming Criteria

A good name should be:

- professional
- easy to say
- easy to spell
- not too long
- suitable for SaaS/product branding
- suitable for subdomains
- not overly Clean Eats-specific
- not too generic
- credible for food/manufacturing operations
- flexible enough for future modules
- available as a domain if possible

Avoid:

- names that sound like a small internal tool
- names that are too cute
- names that only work for meal prep
- names that imply one feature only, such as just inventory or just recipes
- names that are hard to spell
- names that are too close to major existing software brands

## Naming Direction Options

### A. Food-specific names

Examples:

- FoodOps Hub
- FoodFlow
- FoodWorks
- FoodOpsHQ
- Production Pantry
- KitchenOps

Pros:

- clear industry focus
- easy for food businesses to understand

Cons:

- may limit expansion into non-food industries

### B. Production/manufacturing names

Examples:

- BatchFlow
- ProdFlow
- FactoryFlow
- BatchOps
- ProductionHub
- FlowWorks

Pros:

- works for food manufacturing and adjacent industries
- stronger operational feel

Cons:

- may be less immediately food-specific

### C. Operations/control names

Examples:

- OpsHub
- OpsFlow
- SupplyCore
- FlowCore
- ProcessHub
- ControlHub

Pros:

- broad SaaS feel
- works across industries

Cons:

- may be too generic or already taken

### D. Premium/brandable invented names

Examples:

- Fabrik
- KitchCore
- Batchly
- Prodly
- Flowry
- Opero

Pros:

- more brandable
- easier to own if available

Cons:

- may require more explanation

## Recommended Naming Direction

Recommend a direction, not a final name.

Prioritise names that balance food industry clarity with future manufacturing flexibility.

Top direction:

- BatchFlow
- FoodOps Hub
- ProdFlow
- FactoryFlow

These feel more commercially usable than "Food Prod Hub" while still describing production operations.

## Domain Strategy

Future domain targets:

Preferred:

- `.com.au` for Australian launch
- `.com` if available for broader future
- matching app/admin subdomains

Examples:

- `www.name.com.au`
- `app.name.com.au`
- `admin.name.com.au`
- `cleaneats.name.com.au`

Need to check:

- domain availability
- social handles if relevant
- company/trademark conflicts
- pronunciation/spelling
- whether plural/similar names exist

Do not check availability in this task unless explicitly asked later.

## Subdomain Fit

The chosen name should work well in tenant subdomains.

Examples:

- `cleaneats.batchflow.com.au`
- `cleaneats.foodopshub.com.au`
- `cleaneats.prodflow.com.au`

Consider:

- length
- readability
- professional appearance
- client confidence

## Visual Brand Direction

The brand should feel:

- clean
- operational
- modern
- trustworthy
- food-safe / manufacturing-safe
- practical, not gimmicky
- SaaS-quality but not corporate-bloated

Visual cues:

- clean white/light interface
- green/blue/neutral palette options
- modular cards
- operational icons
- flow/connection motifs
- batch/production line motifs
- simple geometric logo possibilities

Tenant branding should still override tenant HUB colours/logo where needed.

The global platform brand should not clash with tenant branding.

## Public Website Brand Needs

Future public website should communicate:

- who it is for
- what modules exist
- why industry-specific beats generic ERP
- Clean Eats case study later
- demo request/sign-up
- module packages
- credibility and trust

## Naming Decision Process

Recommended steps:

1. Generate shortlist.
2. Remove names that are hard to spell/say.
3. Check domain availability.
4. Check existing software/company conflicts.
5. Check if name works in subdomains.
6. Say it out loud in sales/email contexts.
7. Review with Tony/trusted contacts if useful.
8. Pick working commercial name.
9. Secure domain(s).
10. Then start brand/logo exploration.
